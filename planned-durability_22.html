Regulatory Pressure

Europe is moving from “replace” to “last longer”

EU Right to Repair (2025) — manufacturers must offer repairs at fair prices, provide spare parts, and not obstruct third-party repair.

France & Belgium Repairability Index — on-shelf scores requiring documented repairability data for product categories.

EPR Eco-modulation — producer fees reduced for more repairable and durable products, penalising throwaway design.

Ecodesign & Digital Product Passport — minimum durability, reliability data, and full material transparency requirements incoming.

05 — Case Study
Product example

Outdoor IP Camera
Repairability Redesign

Teardown — component mapping and failure point identification

The goal of this project was to increase the repairability and durability of an outdoor IP camera with IP65 certification, Wifi connectivity, and 128GB storage — without compromising weatherproofing or functionality.

Product
Outdoor IP Camera
IP Rating
IP65
Focus
Repairability & Durability
Output
Redesign + Prototype
Durability

Failure analysis identified undersized seals as the primary leak point. The redesign enlarged seal surface area, resolving weatherproofing failures and extending product lifetime significantly.

Redesign

Redundant internal volume was eliminated without affecting IP65 rating. Permanent snap-fits were replaced with tool-accessible fasteners and removable internal cables.

Result

All original specifications maintained while scoring significantly higher on repairability indices — positioning the manufacturer ahead of incoming EU regulation.
